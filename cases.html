CONTRACT LAW COMMON LAW PRECEDENT

Carlill v Carbolic Smoke Ball Co EWCA Civ 1

Core Holding: This landmark decision demonstrates that unilateral contracts can be made to the entire world. The court ruled that advertising an award acts as an offer capable of acceptance by anyone fulfilling the condition, and depositing funds in a bank proved serious intent to create legal relations, negating claims of a mere "sales puff".

Key Takeaway for Startups: Be extremely cautious with public marketing language. Publicly promising explicit refunds, guarantees, or bounties for completing a digital task can bind your company to a strict unilateral contract under common law.
COMMERCIAL DISPUTES LIABILITY LIMITATIONS

Hadley v Baxendale (1854) 9 Exch 341

Core Holding: Established the baseline rule for recovering consequential contract damages. A breaching party is only liable for damages that could reasonably be considered to arise naturally from the breach itself, or damages that were explicitly communicated and mutually contemplated at the time the agreement was formed.

Key Takeaway for Startups: Always explicitly include a "Limitation of Liability" and "Consequential Damages Waiver" clause in your B2B SaaS or consulting agreements to shield your business from massive unpredicted revenue loss.
